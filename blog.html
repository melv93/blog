End of Term and Passwords

  • End of Term!

    Finally, this term is over. Not sure it'll be the most restful break ever but at least a chance to not be in school for a couple of weeks. A few lie in's here and there wouldn't go amis either. Sleep is, as always, greatly missed!

    Nothing is particularly ready for the beginning of next term which does mean some work to be done over this holiday but shouldn't require too much brainpower or effort to complete in time.

  • Passwords

    So I managed to make a password encrypter. Any half decent hacker worth their salt (pun intended) could break it in 30 seconds flat but still! A little bit of salting and some Caeser Cypher and the hash result is quite pleasing, even if the password is reverse engineerable.

    I can't quite get my head around how a password would not be reverse engineerable as any algorithm can usually be worked backwards. This must somehow involve a many to 1 relationship that has only one possible encrypting result but multiple decryption results without reducing the number of possible results by a significant factor.

    Something to look at in the future maybe.

Well this has been a relatively long one. Still can't work out why the matrices program won't load on the school system and need to test it out on another computer at some point to see if it is school security or something wrong with the coding. When I'm home maybe.
